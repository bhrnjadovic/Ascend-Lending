Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

A full doc loan requires complete, verifiable financial documentation — typically two years of business and personal tax returns, accountant-prepared profit and loss statements, balance sheets, and ATO portal access for income confirmation. This gives lenders the comprehensive picture of your business's financial performance they need to underwrite at the highest loan amounts and most competitive rates.

Any Australian business that has been trading for 2+ years with accountant-prepared financials qualifies to apply via the full doc pathway. Qualification for a specific loan amount and rate then depends on your revenue, profitability, asset position, and credit history. The full doc pathway is simply the documentation route — the loan terms that result depend on the underlying financial strength of your business.

Standard full doc requirements include: last 2 years business tax returns, last 2 years personal tax returns for all directors/guarantors, accountant-prepared profit and loss and balance sheets, last 6 months business bank statements, ATO portal confirmation (lodgement and payment status), and for secured loans, property ownership evidence and current council rates notices. We'll provide a precise checklist for your specific scenario.

The rate differential between full doc and low doc varies by lender and loan type, but full doc rates are consistently lower. On larger commercial loans, the difference can be 1–2% per annum — which at $1M represents $10,000–$20,000 in annual interest savings. The exact saving depends on your loan amount, security position, and which lenders we're comparing. We'll show you the specific differential on your scenario.

Full doc lending generally supports LVRs up to 80% on residential property security and 65–70% on commercial property, subject to the lender and your financial position. Some lenders will go higher for very strong borrowers. This is typically 5–15% more leverage than the same scenario presented on a low doc basis, which can be the difference between a deal being viable or not on larger transactions.

Full doc applications involve more thorough underwriting, which takes longer than a low doc or unsecured assessment. Typical timelines run 10–20 business days from application to settlement for secured commercial loans, depending on the lender's current assessment queue, valuation turnaround, and any title or legal requirements. Ascend Lending Partners actively manages each stage to minimise delays and keep your transaction on track.
